Model Note

What this algorithm is trying to prevent

The scoring model is intentionally conservative. It is designed to avoid calling the experiment a full success too early, especially before refund windows and later rounds have tested holder behavior.

In other words, the model rewards commitment and breadth, but it also keeps uncertainty alive until the phase structure matures enough to deserve stronger confidence.

Inputs

What the model reads

The model reads retained capital, participant breadth, wallet concentration, late-phase momentum, and stage maturity. It does not treat total raised alone as proof of success.

Method

How the score is built

Each signal is converted into a normalized component score, then combined with weights. The result is capped by phase so early rounds cannot receive a false full-success reading.

Success score

What a high score means

A higher score means the structure is currently retaining capital, avoiding destructive concentration, and sustaining enough demand to support the commitment thesis at this stage.

Failure risk

What the risk score means

Failure risk rises when exits appear, concentration worsens, later activity fades, or the experiment is simply too early to justify confidence. Early-stage uncertainty is intentionally priced in.
